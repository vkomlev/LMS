# Travel Format Playbook

Use this reference after loading the platform template and `travel-tone.md`.
The template remains authoritative for limits and required structure.

## `tg`

1. Choose one place, detail, event, or practical problem rather than surveying
   an entire destination.
2. Open with a supported scene, specific observation, or useful tension.
3. Develop it in one to three short paragraphs.
4. End with one practical instruction or a concrete thought worth saving.
5. Use Telegram formatting sparingly: bold only where useful, blank lines
   between paragraphs, no more than two emoji.

## `vk`

1. Put the strongest scene or fact in the first 350 characters.
2. Move through scene, context, practical detail, and conclusion.
3. End with a specific question only when engagement is part of the brief.
4. Add three to five relevant hashtags. Do not use generic tags such as
   `#красота`, `#мир`, or `#путешествия` without a narrower destination tag.
5. Return plain text unless the current VK template says otherwise.

## `article`

1. Write an SEO-aware title with the destination or topic and a real promise,
   without clickbait.
2. Open with a scene, contradiction, or concrete problem in two or three
   sentences.
3. Use three to eight H2 sections. Each section needs its own useful point,
   not a rewritten encyclopedia paragraph.
4. Weave logistics into the narrative, but keep critical operational facts
   easy to find.
5. Cover seasonality, including when the trip is a poor fit.
6. Include one supported anti-recommendation when evidence justifies it.
7. Finish with a concrete reconsideration, not a generic wish.
8. Add a 150-160 character meta description only when the template or brief
   requires it.

## `story`

1. Choose one chronological or emotional arc.
2. Open inside a specific moment rather than with background.
3. Develop through supported detail, contrast, friction, or changed
   expectation.
4. Keep practical material secondary.
5. Let the ending alter the meaning of the opening or leave a precise image.
6. Do not fabricate a narrator's personal memory.

## `guide`

1. Cover: why go, when to go, what to see, where to eat when sourced, how to
   move around, and what to avoid.
2. Give each section a point of view or mini-scene; do not produce a bare
   directory of names and addresses.
3. Include a lesser-known place only when it can be verified.
4. Keep prices, hours, addresses, routes, and booking caveats current and
   source-backed.
5. Add an anti-recommendation only when it is reasoned and audience-specific,
   not as manufactured controversy.

## `rewrite`

1. Preserve all supported facts and the author's intended meaning.
2. Remove travel cliches, AI markers, repetition, and unsupported certainty.
3. Adapt structure and length to the requested destination format.
4. Do not introduce new facts merely to make the rewrite more vivid.

## Final Gate

- The first lines earn attention without clickbait.
- Every concrete claim is supplied, verified, omitted, or marked `[уточнить]`.
- The text does not claim personal experience without a source.
- Platform limits and formatting are satisfied.
- The complete `human-quality.md` checklist was applied.
- The final response contains only the requested publication text.
